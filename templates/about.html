Conservation Challenges / NgƒÅ Wero Taiao

New Zealand's wildlife faces numerous challenges that make conservation efforts more critical than ever:

Introduced Predators

Mammals like stoats, possums, and rats threaten native birds that evolved without natural predators.

Habitat Loss

Urban development and agricultural expansion continue to reduce available habitat for native species.

Climate Change

Changing weather patterns affect breeding cycles and food availability for many species.

Human Impact

Pollution, vehicle strikes, and disturbance of nesting sites pose ongoing threats to wildlife populations.

Get Involved / Whakauru Mai

There are many ways you can help protect New Zealand's wildlife:

Support Conservation Organizations: Donate to or volunteer with groups like Forest & Bird, WWF New Zealand, or local wildlife trusts.
Practice Responsible Tourism: When visiting wildlife areas, follow guidelines to minimize disturbance to animals and their habitats.
Create Wildlife-Friendly Spaces: Plant native species in your garden and remove predators to support local wildlife.
Stay Informed: Use our database to learn about species in your area and their conservation needs.
